2014-2015 Admission Process

(8-12th Grade Students New to CES)


By February 14th:
  • Complete application and return it with a check (made payable to Christ Episcopal School) in the amount of $100. Please note the application fee is non-refundable.
  • Submit the following school records: copy of most recent report card and standardized test.
  • Submit a letter (1 page) from applicant to our Admission Committee indicating why student wants to attend Christ Episcopal School.
  • Schedule an entrance exam.
  • Upon completion of the entrance exam, contact the upper school office at 871-9902, ext. 270 to schedule a shadow day and an interview with the High School Division Head.
By Mid-February:
  • Student is notified of acceptance via mail. Once tuition is determined, an enrollment contract is issued. Parents have two (2) weeks from acceptance date to return signed contract with the enrollment deposit as outlined below in the Schedule of Fees.
Once Accepted:
  • The accepted applicant will take a placement exam in Mathematics, English and Spanish (if necessary) in May or August prior to scheduling classes.
At Any Time:
  • Please do not hesitate to contact Pam Perry at 871-9902, ext. 208 with questions you may have.
Schedule of Fees

Note: The fees listed below are based on the 2013-14 school year. Tuition is determined on an annual basis in January by the Board of Trustees. Tuition typically increases between 3%-5%. Each family is assessed a $425 facilities maintenance fee and $175 per student technology fee.

  • 8th - $9,200
  • High School - $10,650

Once a student is notified, an enrollment contract will be issued. The $350 ($150 if applying for financial aid) non-refundable registration deposit (which is part of the tuition) and the signed contract are due two weeks after the acceptance date. The signed contract and registration deposit guarantee the applicant a place in next year's class. Parental obligation is irrevocable effective June 15th unless notification is received in writing prior to that date.

For a better understanding of tuition, fees and financial aid breakdown please visit our Tuition and Financial Aid page.
